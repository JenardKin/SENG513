Point Guard

First one to touch the ball, first one back on defense.

This position houses some of the most skilled players to ever play the game. With the breath taking handles, the dead eye marksmanship, the lightning quick hesitations, and the threading-the-needle passes, the point guard position usually is the most exciting position to watch as they are often the most relatable to the majority of the people watching, seemingly due to them being the smallest out of all the positions played. It was my position when I played in High School but I certainly was not exciting to watch so I set out to be a Software Engineer instead. Nevertheless, their role is pivotal to the team's success.

Roles and Responsibilities

You are most likely the best at handling the ball in your lineup so it will be in your hands most of the time (may vary). You can do a left-to-right crossover, a right-to-left behind the back, an in and out dribble in transition, a Pro-hop euro step, a step-back pull up turnaround fadeaway jumper with peanut butter jelly on the top. These are the moves up in your arsenal and you are not afraid to use them. There are also game plans and sets drawn out by each team to score and defend easier. Your job as the main ball handler is to make sure they are executed well. Communicating and assisting your team members to remember where they are supposed to be is a responsibility that all point guards must fulfill. Failure to find an opportunity for your team to score in the half court setting, or in transition, will be on you (well if your team sucks, you cant do much) and will result in your coach yelling at your ear.

Since on average, they are the smallest ones on the court, they tend to stay around the perimeter and away from the basket. If you are as quick as lightning, can bulldoze through multiple defenders or have a 48 inch vertical jump, then by all means, drive the ball to the hoop, no one is stopping you. Im just saying that you will get blocked amongst the tall Centers if you don't. Thus, the best way to score would be to shoot behind the three point line just like Stephen Curry.
