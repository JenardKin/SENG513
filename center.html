Center

A Man amongst boys.

You know the old saying, "somewhere out there, there is always someone better than you." Well in the NBA, instead its there's someone "bigger" than you. This is the case when it comes to the Center position. They are the biggest and tallest amongst all the players. Although not known for their freakish athleticism such as speed and quickness, they make up for it in terms of strength, wingspan, and well, height. They can rebound, block shots, set screens, protect the paint, sometimes even shoot the three and handle the ball, and usually finish around the rim either through a lob, hook shot, post fadeaways, and emphatic dunks.

Roles and Responsibilities

Layups and dunks in basketball are like eating cookies, they are both extremely easy. With you being the tallest amongst your teammates, you are responsible for making sure that the opposing team does not score many easy baskets by protecting the paint. On offense, you are technically closest to the hoop as you can probably touch it without even jumping and just simply raising your hands. Thus, you are the go-to scorer when the team is unable to score from the perimeter and in need of an easy basket. Due to your size, you would also be a good screen setter. Defenders would easily be caught on you just by standing still.

Having said all of that, once you step out of the paint area and near the perimeter, that is when you begin to feel like "Ok, they are pretty fast." On a mismatch, especially out in the perimeter, you will get beat by quicker and more skilled guards and forwards. So remember, dont stray far from the hoop, otherwise you will be left in an island or worse, get your ankles broken!
