Small Forward

Versatility against multiple positions.

There is nothing small about the skill set, physical prowess, and impact of the players playing this position when it comes to winning basketball games. The Small Forward position packs a versatile all-around bunch who can light you up with three pointers and cross you over like a point guard, shoot over or drive past you like an off guard, school you in the post with a dreamlike up and under moves like a power forward, and defend the rim and get every single rebound like a Center. In other words, you are a generalist and have the ability as well as the responsibility to do it all in the basketball court.

Roles and Responsibilities

You are expected to have the skillset of multiple positions when it comes to being a small forward. Whatever it is that your team's lineup currently lacks is basically what you will have to offer. Whenever the shooting guard is not pulling his weight on offense, you are the second option to go to (unless you also suck) when it comes to scoring. Your skillset and physicality combined allows you to drive past your oppponent or shoot over them using your height. From dunks to layups, to freethrow shooting, and three pointers, you can be the offensive anchor for you team. As for rebounding, it would be second nature to you especially if your Center or Power Forward likes shooting threes and feels like they are a Point Guard. You can go deep in your bag of tricks, maybe with your dribble set, your layup and post play package, and maybe with your passing prominence to win games. You can either follow the lead of the guards in transition, or start it on your own and be a one man wrecking crew due to your speed and size.

With that said, there is really not a special responsibility that a small forward covers that cannot be done better than those of the other positions. This makes them to be classified as being good enough to handle most things but not really as spectacular as the others. However on defense, you can be the main perimeter defender. You can shut down their primary scorer if coach demands you to do so. You can soar high for blocks and rebounds, be quick enough to stay on slashing guards, and be mindful enough to anticipate play sets and passing lanes of the opposing team.
